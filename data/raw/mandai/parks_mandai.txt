Mandai Wildlife Reserve – Parks & Presentations (Placeholder)

1) Singapore Zoo
Summary:
A large, open-concept zoo featuring animals from multiple continents in naturalistic habitats, with a focus on education and conservation.

Key Presentations / Shows (example times):
- Breakfast in the Wild – 9:00am
  A breakfast experience with views of animal habitats and occasional keeper sharing sessions.
- Rainforest Encounters – 11:30am
  A guided presentation introducing rainforest mammals and birds.
- Into the Wild – 2:00pm
  Stage-style show highlighting animal behaviours and conservation messages.
- KidzWorld Splash Time – 4:00pm
  Children-focused interactive segment with domestic animals and play elements.

2) River Wonders
Summary:
A river-themed park showcasing freshwater ecosystems and river-dependent wildlife from around the world.

Key Presentations / Shows:
- Once Upon a River – 11:00am and 3:00pm
  A narrated show featuring river animals such as otters, capybaras, and fish species.
- Amazon River Quest Boat Ride – Operates throughout the day
  A slow boat ride past themed animal zones inspired by South American river habitats.
- River Keepers Talk – 1:30pm
  Short talk by animal care staff about feeding and enrichment for aquatic species.

3) Night Safari
Summary:
A nocturnal wildlife park that opens after dark, focusing on animals most active at night, with walking trails and tram rides.

Key Presentations / Shows:
- Creatures of the Night – 7:45pm and 9:15pm
  Main night-time presentation highlighting nocturnal animals and their adaptations.
- Safari Tram Narrated Ride – Multiple departures between 7:30pm and 11:00pm
  Guided tram ride through dimly lit habitats.
- After Dark Discovery Walk – 10:00pm
  Guided trail walk introducing lesser-known nocturnal species.

4) Bird Paradise
Summary:
A bird-focused park with large aviaries, walk-through habitats, and breeding areas for rare and colourful bird species.

Key Presentations / Shows:
- Wings of the World – 12:30pm and 5:00pm
  Stage presentation featuring free-flying birds, training demonstrations, and conservation messages.
- Sky Flyers – 11:00am
  Outdoor flight demonstration showing raptors and other strong fliers.
- Feathered Friends Chat – 3:30pm
  Informal keeper talk about parrot species, hornbills, and other aviary inhabitants.

5) Rainforest Wild Adventure
Summary:
An adventure-style rainforest attraction combining rope courses, viewpoints, and selected animal exhibits for a more immersive experience.

Key Presentations / Shows:
- Canopy Trek Briefing – hourly from 10:00am to 4:00pm
  Safety and orientation briefing before guests embark on canopy rope courses.
- Rainforest Rangers – 1:00pm
  Short talk introducing key rainforest species seen along the adventure routes.
- Sunset Lookout Storytime – 6:00pm
  Storytelling session about rainforest conservation and exploration history.
