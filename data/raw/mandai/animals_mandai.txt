Mandai Wildlife Reserve – Animals & Facts (Placeholder)

Singapore Zoo

1) African Lion
- Fact 1: Lions live in social groups called prides, often led by related females.
- Fact 2: A male lion’s mane helps protect its neck during fights and makes it look larger to rivals.
- Fact 3: Many wild lion populations are declining due to habitat loss and human–wildlife conflict.

2) Asian Elephant
- Fact 1: Asian elephants have smaller ears than African elephants and a more rounded back.
- Fact 2: They use their trunks for breathing, smelling, drinking, and manipulating objects.
- Fact 3: Asian elephants are considered endangered because of habitat loss and fragmentation.

3) White Tiger
- Fact 1: White tigers are Bengal tigers with a genetic variation affecting fur colour.
- Fact 2: Their stripes are unique to each individual, much like human fingerprints.
- Fact 3: White tigers are rare in the wild and are mostly found in managed care settings.

River Wonders

4) Giant Panda
- Fact 1: Giant pandas have a diet dominated by bamboo, despite being classified as carnivores.
- Fact 2: Their black-and-white fur may help with camouflage and temperature regulation.
- Fact 3: Intensive conservation programs have helped improve their global conservation status.

5) Manatee
- Fact 1: Manatees are large, slow-moving aquatic mammals that graze on plants.
- Fact 2: They must surface regularly to breathe but can stay underwater for several minutes.
- Fact 3: Boat strikes in shallow waters are a major threat to wild manatee populations.

6) Capybara
- Fact 1: Capybaras are the world’s largest rodents and live in social groups near water.
- Fact 2: They are strong swimmers and often enter water to escape predators or cool off.
- Fact 3: Capybaras have semi-webbed feet that help them move efficiently in marshy terrain.

Night Safari

7) Malayan Tapir
- Fact 1: Malayan tapirs have a distinctive black-and-white pattern that breaks up their outline at night.
- Fact 2: They have a short, flexible snout used to grab leaves and sniff their surroundings.
- Fact 3: They are shy, nocturnal forest dwellers threatened by habitat loss.

8) Clouded Leopard
- Fact 1: Clouded leopards have large, cloud-shaped markings on their fur.
- Fact 2: They are excellent climbers and can descend trees head-first.
- Fact 3: Their long canine teeth are proportionally among the longest of any cat species.

9) Binturong
- Fact 1: Binturongs are sometimes called “bearcats” but are neither bears nor cats.
- Fact 2: Their prehensile tails help them balance and grip branches as they move in trees.
- Fact 3: Their scent can resemble buttered popcorn due to specific chemical compounds.

Bird Paradise

10) Scarlet Macaw
- Fact 1: Scarlet macaws are large, brightly coloured parrots native to Central and South America.
- Fact 2: They have strong beaks capable of cracking hard nuts and seeds.
- Fact 3: Their loud calls help them communicate across long distances in forest habitats.

11) Hornbill (generic)
- Fact 1: Hornbills have a distinctive casque on top of their beaks, whose function varies among species.
- Fact 2: In many species, females nest in tree cavities sealed with mud and food, leaving only a small opening.
- Fact 3: They play an important role as seed dispersers in tropical forests.

12) Flamingo
- Fact 1: Flamingos get their pink colour from pigments in the algae and crustaceans they eat.
- Fact 2: They often stand on one leg, possibly to conserve body heat.
- Fact 3: Large colonies help protect individuals from predators and aid in finding mates.

Rainforest Wild Adventure

13) Gibbon
- Fact 1: Gibbons are small apes known for brachiation, swinging hand-over-hand through trees.
- Fact 2: Their loud songs help mark territory and strengthen pair bonds.
- Fact 3: Many gibbon species are endangered due to deforestation in Southeast Asia.

14) Malayan Flying Fox (fruit bat)
- Fact 1: Flying foxes are large fruit bats that play a key role in pollination and seed dispersal.
- Fact 2: They navigate primarily by sight and smell rather than echolocation.
- Fact 3: They roost in large colonies during the day and fly out at dusk to feed.

15) Green Iguana
- Fact 1: Green iguanas are arboreal lizards that spend much of their time in trees.
- Fact 2: They are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, flowers, and fruits.
- Fact 3: When threatened, they can drop from trees into water and swim away using their tail.
